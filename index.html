What is the difference between promotional and transactional SMS services?

Promotional and transactional SMS services are two different types of messaging services that businesses can use to communicate with their customers.

Promotional SMS messages are typically used for marketing and advertising purposes. They are sent with the intention of promoting a product, service, or event, and are often delivered to a large audience. Promotional messages can include sales promotions, special offers, and other types of marketing campaigns.

On the other hand, transactional SMS messages are typically used for providing customers with important or time-sensitive information related to their transactions or accounts. These messages are triggered by specific events or actions, such as a password reset, a purchase confirmation, or a delivery update. They are sent to individual customers and are typically personalized to include relevant information specific to the customer’s transaction.

In terms of regulatory compliance, there are often different rules and restrictions governing promotional and transactional SMS messages.

What is a custom Sender ID

A Sender ID is an alphanumeric code that allows you to brand your messages with your company or service name giving your users confidence in the source of the message while at the same time promoting brand visibility. The sender ID should not exceed 11 characters. A generic Sender ID is added to all registered account for a quick message. However, customers have to be KYCed to use custom Sender ID.
